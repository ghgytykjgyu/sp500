A Data-Driven Analysis of Global Markets (2020–2026)






🧠 Executive Summary

This project delivers a quantitative, data-driven analysis of the US–China trade war and its systemic impact on global financial markets, commodities, and macroeconomic indicators.

Rather than relying on narrative-driven headlines, this analysis leverages statistical modeling, time-series analysis, and sentiment data to uncover the real economic dynamics behind one of the most influential geopolitical conflicts of the decade.

✨ Key Insights
📈 Market Resilience vs Panic
S&P 500 gained +115.6% (2020–2026)
Steel futures surged +78%
➡️ Protectionism boosted domestic sectors
⚡ Event-Driven Market Shock (CAR Analysis)
Major tariff events triggered sharp negative abnormal returns
Markets showed strong recovery within ~20 days
➡️ Short-term panic ≠ long-term damage
🔗 Structural Decoupling
Rolling correlation dropped -1.4%
➡️ Evidence of economic divergence between US & China
📰 Sentiment ≠ Reality
News coverage remained mostly neutral
➡️ Market reactions driven by interpretation, not emotion
📦 Trade Deficit Insight
Record -$136B (March 2025)
➡️ Caused by pre-tariff import surge (front-running behavior)
